Early Life

Faqir Chand Kohli, also known as FC Kohli, was born on February 19, 1924, in Peshawar, British India (now Pakistan). He grew up in Lahore and later moved to Mumbai to pursue his education.

Career

Kohli was a pioneer of the Indian IT industry. He was the first CEO of Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), which he joined in 1969. Under his leadership, TCS became one of the largest IT companies in the world.

In 1969, he helped set up the Tata Consultancy Services, at the request of J. R. D. Tata, the group chairman, after Tata Electric company installed a computer system to control the power lines between Mumbai and Pune, making it the third utility company in the world to install such a system.[14] After internally serving a few of the Tata Group companies, TCS went on to sign its first contract with Burroughs Corporation for software services, in 1972.[14][15] He would go on to become the company's first CEO and also serve as its deputy chairman.[7][12][14] He would lead the company for the next three decades before stepping down as the CEO in 1996.[15][16] The company is the largest Indian IT services company by market capitalization and the most valuable company within the Tata group as of 2020.[16]

Kohli also played a key role in setting up the Software Technology Parks of India (STPI), which helped to boost the software industry in India. He was also involved in the creation of the Centre for Development of Advanced Computing (C-DAC), which developed India's first supercomputer, the PARAM 8000.

Legacy

FC Kohli was a visionary leader and a pioneer of the Indian IT industry. He played a crucial role in putting India on the global map of technology and was instrumental in creating a strong foundation for the industry to grow.

Kohli was widely respected for his leadership, vision, and contributions to the IT industry. He received many awards and honors throughout his career, including the Padma Bhushan in 2002, one of India's highest civilian honors.
